## ENGLISH1: Subject and Predicate [Unit]

### Part A - Divide the Sentences into Subject and Predicate

**1.** The box with books is heavier than the one with clothes. **2.** How nice it looks on you! **3.** Do you know that Asia is the largest continent? **4.** Under the tree sat a wise old man. **5.** My grandmother tells me interesting stories every evening. **6.** What fun those days were! **7.** Be sincere and do your classwork properly. **8.** The stars in the night sky twinkled brightly. **9.** Who is that boy crying so bitterly now? **10.** At the bottom of the river, lay his axe. **11.** What a lovely surprise this is! **12.** All the children in the class were excited about the picnic. **13.** Out of the bush, came out a snake. **14.** Do not speak ill of anyone. **15.** Where did the boys go after school? **16.** It is such a pity! **17.** The child who is playing in the street is my best friend. **18.** A big oak tree stood in a corner of the yard. **19.** How silly of me to make this mistake again and again! **20.** The teacher is explaining the lesson. **21.** When will the train arrive at the station? **22.** On the desk, are his books and notebooks. **23.** Bring your homework tomorrow. **24.** How grand this dress looks. **25.** All that hibernate, wake up after a long period. **26.** My friend and her sister play the violin beautifully. **27.** Who brought these beautiful flowers? **28.** Here is the bus stop. **29.** How beautiful your dress looks! **30.** A loud crash woke us up in the middle of the night. **31.** Do not waste the fresh drinking water that comes from the tap. **32.** The students of class five are going on a picnic to the zoo tomorrow. **33.** What a beautiful rainbow it is! **34.** The poor old woman gets little food. **35.** Where did the puppies go? **36.** The old banyan tree near our school provides cool shade in summer. **37.** How wonderful the music sounds! **38.** The little boy with a red cap ran across the field.
