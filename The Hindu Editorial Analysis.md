## SHORT STORY 1 — THE CURIOUS MIND THAT BROKE EVERY BARRIER

Manav was an inquisitive student from a small town who never accepted failure blithely, because every defeat made him ask deeper questions about his purpose, his discipline, and his future; people around him often found his dreams bizarre, especially when he said he wanted to clear one of the toughest competitive exams despite having limited resources, an unwonted level of confidence, and no powerful background to support him. His journey was not easy, for the syllabus looked gargantuan, the pressure was heavy, and many classmates tried to discourage him with loud bombast, claiming that success belonged only to those who studied in famous institutions. But Manav did not capitulate before fear; instead, he turned every insult into fuel and every silent night into preparation. Some people even tried to ostracise him because he refused to waste time in licentious distractions and meaningless pleasures, yet he stayed focused, believing that a disciplined life may look lonely at first but becomes powerful with patience. Slowly, his hard work produced bountiful results: his scores improved, his confidence returned, and the same people who once mocked his ambition began to admire his determination. On the final day, when Manav saw his name on the selection list, he understood that curiosity, courage, and consistency can break every barrier, no matter how strange the beginning looks.

**Moral of the Story:** A curious and disciplined mind can defeat rejection, pressure, and fear. Success comes to those who refuse to surrender before difficulties.

## SHORT STORY 2 — THE ASCETIC MIND AND THE AMBITIOUS DREAM

Manav lived in a crowded town where people often measured success by wealth, noise, and appearance, but he secretly admired the discipline of an ascetic, because he believed that a simple life could protect a serious dream from distraction; his family had once suffered under a cruel usurer, and that painful memory became a black day in his heart, pushing him to study harder so that poverty would never force him to run away from dignity. Every morning, he kept his old pen in a broken metal sheath as if it were a warrior's sword, and he wrote his promises verbatim in a small diary, deciding to put something down in black and white before life could confuse him with fear. Many classmates laughed at him, saying that luxury was the new black and discipline was outdated, but Manav refused to accept those black thoughts; whenever he failed in mock tests, he would run down his mistakes patiently, trace every weak area, and rebuild his preparation with calm courage. One evening, he happened to run into his former teacher near the library, and her words changed his direction: "If you want to run for your future, first learn to stand alone." From that day, even when nights looked black as sin and the road seemed endless, Manav kept working with quiet faith, proving that an ambitious dream does not need a noisy life; it needs a disciplined mind, honest effort, and the courage to rise again after every defeat.

**Moral of the Story:** A simple and disciplined life can protect even the biggest dream from fear, failure, and distraction.

## ARTICLE FOR READING — PERFECT STORM: ON ILLICIT LIQUOR IN INDIA

India has suffered multiple mass deaths due to consuming illicit liquor, across Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Assam, and Maharashtra. The Malwani incident in 2015, which left over 100 people dead, prompted official promises of systemic reform that never fully materialised — neither there nor elsewhere. The Pune-Pimpri-Chinchwad tragedy unfolded last week with more than a dozen victims of working-class backgrounds in poor neighbourhoods. Preliminary investigation has revealed a better than ad hoc supply chain with industry-grade methanol — the toxin behind most hooch tragedies — brought from outside the State and mixed with ethanol to produce a highly potent country liquor. The demand for licensed alcohol faces high State taxes, encouraging low-income individuals to turn to illicit liquor.

On the other hand, adding industrial methanol increases the batch volume at negligible input cost, dramatically improving margins, although some people under investigation have also said during official questioning that it is not in the interest of 'regular' vendors to poison their 'customers'. Nonetheless, such operations are typically semi-visible local economies that get by on tolerance rather than secrecy, and so allegations of police and local authorities' complicity must be investigated. Early enforcement following most deadly incidents only arrests retail vendors; investigations into upstream suppliers and alleged kingpins have frequently proved uneven or inconclusive. Indeed, these tragedies recur due to a perfect storm of factors that are able to repeatedly come together. One major regulatory gap in efforts to track methanol downstream is how easily it is pilfered and diverted for illicit liquor.

Most victims are daily-wage labourers, and scholars have argued that the physical toll of manual labour creates a demand for cheap relief that, together with economic precarity and addiction, outweighs the wariness of poisonous substances. Since the victims are often from marginalised communities, the sustained political will needed to implement reforms — including better methanol accounting and affordable alcohol alternatives, required to break the cycle — is often lacking. In a 2024 analysis, public health experts found that higher prices for legal liquor push the poorest consumers toward the illicit market, which accounts for an estimated 40% of alcohol consumption in India. Likewise, total bans as in Bihar and Gujarat could deflect the market to criminal syndicates, where quality control is optional and oversight is poor. Finally, legal reviews have concluded that while the big fish are rarely caught, even those arrested are rarely convicted. Without these improvements, regulatory loopholes and weak enforcement will sustain illicit liquor as a public health crisis.

**Summary:** India continues to witness recurring hooch tragedies, including the recent Pune-Pimpri-Chinchwad incident, due to a combination of regulatory failures, weak enforcement, and socio-economic factors. Illicit liquor producers use industrial methanol to increase profits, while high taxes on legal alcohol drive low-income consumers toward cheaper, unsafe alternatives. Investigations often target only local vendors, leaving major suppliers unpunished. Poor methanol tracking, political neglect of marginalized victims, and low conviction rates further worsen the problem. Experts estimate that illicit liquor accounts for about 40% of alcohol consumption in India. Stronger regulation, effective enforcement, and affordable legal alternatives are essential to address this public health crisis.

## ARTICLE FOR SKIMMING — JOY AND PAIN: ON THE NFHS-6 DATA

When triumph and disaster arrive at the same time, it is important to celebrate, but also, to take stock, assess, and pivot to address the lacuna. The recently released National Family Health Survey (NFHS)-6 data offer such an opportunity. The data for 2023-24 revealed some remarkable gains for India, but also exposed some unguarded pathways that will become disastrous if not attended to. The greatest of the gains came in the sector of child health — stunting is down by 17%; severe wasting is down 32%; institutional deliveries are at over 90%; and full immunisation coverage for children aged 12-23 months rose to over 87%. Additionally, the figures show that India's Total Fertility Rate (TFR) had stabilised at 2.0, below the replacement level of 2.1. Unequivocally, these are gains; ones that India has been working on for decades. It is an encouraging sign that the needle has finally moved, in these crucial sectors.

These gains will have to be built upon, without any drop in service delivery, while remaining uncompromising on access or quality of services in the public sector. However, health managers need to heed the other side of the paradigm as well. NFHS-6 indicates a 'dual public health burden' — obesity (in three years, it had grown from 22.9% to 27.3% among men, while among women, from 24% to 30.7%) remains problematic while some level of malnutrition continues, besides the onslaught of lifestyle diseases. The survey also showed a decline in exclusive breastfeeding among children under six months, from 63.7% in NFHS-5 to 55.8% in NFHS-6. Breast feeding is essential to prevent infant malnutrition. The NFHS, one of the largest cross-sectional household surveys in the world, could easily be considered the primary tool to define public policy, and evidence-based governance, besides tracking development indicators.

The data it generates are crucial. Other India-level data from similar periods indicate similar trends; the SRS and the National Health Accounts Survey reveal the lack of focus or funds for lifestyle diseases, and metabolic disorders. The danger of letting this continue will be known sooner rather than later, as India progresses through a demographic transition to a greyer nation. At this stage, transformations are still possible. Setting up comprehensive screening programmes for NCDs, stressing on nation-wide behaviour change communication on diet and exercise, and ensuring higher taxes on sugared beverages and packaged foods are all techniques that will help reduce, and limit the burden, of NCDs. Bolstering health systems to tackle these NCDs, once they set in, at every level — village, town and city — is also critical.

## PASSAGE FOR READING COMPREHENSION — THE IRAN WAR AND WORLD ORDER

The ongoing war in Iran has shattered many long-standing assumptions about global stability and exposed the deep structural weaknesses of the existing international order. For decades, the world was led to believe that globalization, economic interdependence, and diplomatic institutions would prevent large-scale conflicts. However, the crisis in Iran has demonstrated that geopolitical rivalries continue to outweigh economic considerations when strategic interests are at stake. One of the most significant lessons from the conflict is the declining effectiveness of international institutions. Organizations that were established to maintain peace and uphold international law have often appeared powerless in preventing escalation. The inability of major powers to reach a consensus has further highlighted the limitations of multilateral diplomacy. The war has also revealed the fragility of global energy security.

Iran's strategic location near the Strait of Hormuz, a vital route for global oil shipments, has shown how regional conflicts can disrupt international markets and trigger economic uncertainty across continents. Rising oil prices and supply chain disruptions have reminded the world that regional wars can have global consequences. Furthermore, the conflict has underscored the emergence of a more fragmented and multipolar world. Regional powers, non-state actors, and strategic alliances have played crucial roles, reducing the ability of any single superpower to dictate outcomes. The crisis reflects a shift from a predictable international system to one characterized by competing interests and unstable balances of power. Ultimately, the war in Iran serves as a warning that peace cannot be sustained merely through economic ties or military deterrence.

**Question 1:** How has the Iran war exposed weaknesses in the existing world order?

**Answer:** The Iran war has exposed the inability of international institutions to effectively prevent conflicts and maintain peace. It has demonstrated that geopolitical rivalries often override diplomatic efforts and economic interdependence. The conflict has also highlighted the limitations of global governance structures, as major powers remain divided on critical issues. As a result, the war has raised concerns about the effectiveness, credibility, and relevance of the current international order.

**Question 2:** Why is the Strait of Hormuz important in the context of the Iran conflict?

**Answer:** The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most important oil transit routes, carrying a significant portion of global energy supplies. Any disruption caused by the Iran conflict can affect international oil markets, increase fuel prices, and create economic instability worldwide. The war has demonstrated how a regional conflict can quickly produce global consequences, emphasizing the interconnected nature of energy security, trade, and international economic stability.

**Topic:** India–Canada Relations: From Challenges to Opportunities

India and Canada, two vibrant democracies with strong people-to-people ties, stand at an important crossroads in their bilateral relationship. Over the years, cooperation in trade, education, technology, and cultural exchanges has strengthened the bond between the two nations. However, recent diplomatic tensions have created challenges that require careful handling and renewed engagement. The relationship between India and Canada is built on shared democratic values, respect for diversity, and a commitment to the rule of law. Canada is home to a large Indian diaspora that contributes significantly to its economy and society while also acting as a bridge between the two countries. Educational partnerships and student mobility have further deepened these connections, making the relationship more than just a matter of diplomacy.

Despite these strengths, political disagreements and security concerns have strained ties in recent years. Such differences, if left unresolved, can affect trade, investment, and broader strategic cooperation. Therefore, both countries must focus on dialogue, mutual respect, and constructive diplomacy to rebuild trust and confidence. Economic cooperation offers a promising pathway forward. India's growing economy and Canada's strengths in natural resources, clean energy, agriculture, and advanced technology provide ample opportunities for collaboration. Expanding trade agreements, encouraging investments, and promoting innovation partnerships can generate benefits for both nations. Furthermore, cooperation on global issues such as climate change, sustainable development, and international security can help create a stronger and more resilient partnership.

As influential members of the global community, India and Canada have much to gain from working together in multilateral forums. The next chapter in India–Canada relations should be guided by pragmatism, shared interests, and a long-term vision. By addressing differences through dialogue and expanding areas of cooperation, both nations can transform current challenges into opportunities and build a partnership that contributes to prosperity, stability, and global progress.
