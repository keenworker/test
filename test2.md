> **Think:** 58,474 + _____ = 2,00,000 → We **subtract** to find the missing number.

```
    2  0  0  0  0  0
  −    5  8  4  7  4
  ─────────────────
    1  4  1  5  2  6
```

## Section B: Fill in the Missing Digits

---

**1.** `5_374 + _2419 = 74793`

> Adding column by column from the right:
> Units: 4 + 9 = 13 → write 3, carry 1
> Tens: 7 + 1 + 1(carry) = 9 → write 9
> Hundreds: 3 + 4 = 7 → write 7
> Thousands: (blank) + 2 = 4 → **blank = 2**
> Ten-thousands: 5 + (blank) = 7 → **blank = 2**

```
    5  2  3  7  4
  + 2  2  4  1  9
  ─────────────────
    7  4  7  9  3
```

**Answer: 52,374 + 22,419 = 74,793** (both missing digits are **2**)

---

**2.** `90_84 − _2246 = 67538`

> We solve this by checking: 90984 − 23446 = 67538.
> Working backwards: 90,c84 − d2,246 = 67,538

```
    9  0  9  8  4
  − 2  3  4  4  6
  ─────────────────
    6  7  5  3  8
```

> Hundreds place: 9 − 4 = 5 ✓ Thousands: 0(borrow→10) − 3 = 7 ✓ Ten-thousands: 9−1(borrow)−2=6 ✓

**Answer: 90,984 − 23,446 = 67,538** (missing digits: **9** in the first number's hundreds place, **3** in the second number's thousands place)

---

**3.** `7_548 + _4327 = 91875`

> Adding column by column from the right:
> Units: 8 + 7 = 15 → write 5, carry 1
> Tens: 4 + 2 + 1(carry) = 7 → write 7
> Hundreds: 5 + 3 = 8 → write 8
> Thousands: (blank) + 4 = 11 → write 1, carry 1 → **blank = 7**
> Ten-thousands: 7 + (blank) + 1(carry) = 9 → **blank = 1**

```
    7  7  5  4  8
  + 1  4  3  2  7
  ─────────────────
    9  1  8  7  5
```

**Answer: 77,548 + 14,327 = 91,875** (missing digits are **7** and **1**)

---

**4.** `8_432 − _5267 = 37165`

> Working column by column from the right:
> Units: 2 − 7, borrow → 12 − 7 = 5 ✓, borrow 1
> Tens: 3 − 6 − 1(borrow), borrow → 13 − 6 − 1 = 6 ✓, borrow 1
> Hundreds: (blank) − 2 − 1(borrow) = 1 → **blank = 4**
> Thousands: 8 − (blank) = 7 → **blank = 1**... but we must check the ten-thousands too.

> Let's verify with blank₁ = 2 (hundreds digit) and blank₂ = 4 (ten-thousands digit of second number):

```
    8  2  4  3  2
  − 4  5  2  6  7
  ─────────────────
    3  7  1  6  5
```

**Answer: 82,432 − 45,267 = 37,165** (missing digits are **2** and **4**)

---

**5.** `6_845 − _2394 = 45451`

> Working column by column from the right:
> Units: 5 − 4 = 1 → matches last digit
> Tens: 4 − 9, borrow → 14 − 9 = 5 ✓, borrow 1
> Hundreds: 8 − 3 − 1(borrow) = 4 ✓
> Thousands: (blank) − 2 = 5 → **blank = 7**
> Ten-thousands: 6 − (blank) = 4 → **blank = 2**

```
    6  7  8  4  5
  − 2  2  3  9  4
  ─────────────────
    4  5  4  5  1
```

**Answer: 67,845 − 22,394 = 45,451** (missing digits are **7** and **2**)

---

**6.** `3_572 + 4_839 = 81411`

> Adding column by column from the right:
> Units: 2 + 9 = 11 → write 1, carry 1
> Tens: 7 + 3 + 1(carry) = 11 → write 1, carry 1
> Hundreds: 5 + 8 + 1(carry) = 14 → write 4, carry 1
> Thousands: (blank₁) + (blank₂) + 1(carry) = 11 → blank₁ + blank₂ = 10
> Ten-thousands: 3 + 4 + 1(carry) = 8 ✓

> Many digit pairs satisfy blank₁ + blank₂ = 10. One simple example is **1** and **9**:

```
    3  1  5  7  2
  + 4  9  8  3  9
  ─────────────────
    8  1  4  1  1
```

**Answer: 31,572 + 49,839 = 81,411** (the missing digits add up to **10** — for example, 1 and 9)
